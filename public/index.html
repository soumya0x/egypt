Ancient Egypt
Itinerary

Pyramids, temples, tombs, and the slow drift of the Nile. Pick your pace — five to fourteen days from Cairo to Abu Simbel.

The essentials. Cairo, Giza, Luxor. Tight but covers the headline sites — for travelers short on time who still want the pyramids and a temple crawl.

DAY 01Arrival & Cairo Cairo

  • Morning: Arrive Cairo International. Hotel check-in, rest.
  • Afternoon: Egyptian Museum — Tutankhamun's mask, royal mummies hall.
  • Evening: Dinner in Zamalek, Nile riverfront walk.
Museum moved most collection to GEM (Grand Egyptian Museum) near Giza — check what's open before going.

DAY 02Giza Pyramids Giza

  • Sunrise: Great Pyramid, Khafre, Menkaure. Optional entry into any pyramid.
  • Late morning: Solar Boat Museum.
  • Afternoon: Great Sphinx & Valley Temple. Sound & Light show in the evening.
Camel ride at the panhandle — agree on price before mounting. Sunrise beats midday (cooler, fewer crowds).

DAY 03Fly to Luxor Luxor

  • Flight: Cairo → Luxor (~1h).
  • East Bank: Karnak Temple complex, Luxor Temple (lit at night).
  • Evening: Hotel on the West Bank.

DAY 04West Bank Luxor

  • Morning: Valley of the Kings — three tombs included in ticket (Tut, Seti I, Ramses V/VI worth the extra).
  • Midday: Hatshepsut's temple at Deir el-Bahari.
  • Afternoon: Colossi of Memnon, then Medinet Habu (Ramses III mortuary temple — underrated).
Photography forbidden in most tombs. Tut's tomb costs extra but is the only one with wall paintings fully intact.

DAY 05Fly Home Luxor → Cairo

  • Morning: Hot-air balloon over the West Bank (optional, ~$80).
  • Flight: Luxor → Cairo → home, or direct Luxor departure.

The classic. Adds Aswan, Philae, and a Nile felucca — the sweet spot for first-timers who want temples and atmosphere.

DAY 01–02Cairo & Giza Cairo

  • Day 1: Old Cairo, Coptic Quarter, Khan el-Khalili bazaar.
  • Day 2: Giza plateau at sunrise, Sphinx, GEM if open.

DAY 03Fly to Luxor, East Bank Luxor

  • Karnak Temple, Luxor Temple illuminated at night.

DAY 04West Bank Luxor

  • Valley of the Kings, Hatshepsut, Medinet Habu, Colossi of Memnon.

DAY 05Drive to Aswan Aswan

  • ~3.5h drive or train. Check in, rest.
  • Sunset felucca around Elephantine Island.

DAY 06Aswan & Philae Aswan

  • Morning: Philae Temple (Isis, relocated to Agilkia Island).
  • Afternoon: Unfinished Obelisk, High Dam, optional Nubian village visit.
Philae at golden hour beats midday — bring a tripod if you shoot.

DAY 07Fly Home Aswan → Cairo

  • Early flight via Cairo, or stay one more night in Aswan if jet-lagged.

The classics plus a 3-night Nile cruise. Slow the pace, add Edfu, Kom Ombo, and time on the water.

DAY 01–02Cairo Cairo

  • Egyptian Museum / GEM, Old Cairo, Khan el-Khalili.
  • Saladin Citadel, Muhammad Ali Mosque, panoramic city views.

DAY 03Giza & Memphis Giza · Saqqara

  • Pyramids of Giza, Sphinx, Solar Boat.
  • Drive to Saqqara: Step Pyramid of Djoser (oldest stone building in the world).

DAY 04Luxor, Embark Luxor

  • Fly Cairo → Luxor. Karnak Temple.
  • Board Nile cruise, lunch on board, Luxor Temple at night.

DAY 05West Bank Luxor

  • Valley of the Kings, Hatshepsut, Medinet Habu, Colossi of Memnon.
  • Cruise departs Luxor for Edfu overnight.

DAY 06Edfu & Kom Ombo On the Nile

  • Morning: Horus Temple at Edfu (horse-carriage ride from dock).
  • Afternoon: Kom Ombo — unique double temple (Sobek & Horus the Elder). Crocodile museum.

DAY 07Aswan Aswan

  • Philae Temple, High Dam, Unfinished Obelisk.
  • Disembark, hotel in Aswan, Nubian dinner.

DAY 08Abu Simbel (Optional) Aswan · Abu Simbel

  • 3am convoy drive (~3h each way) or fly. Ramesses II's rock-cut temples. Back by evening.
Fly option ($250ish) saves 6h of road. Worth it for a second visit to Egypt later.

DAY 09Return to Cairo Aswan → Cairo

  • Fly Aswan → Cairo. Free afternoon: pool, last-minute souk, dinner cruise on the Nile.

DAY 10Fly Home Cairo

  • Transfer to airport.

The deep dive. Adds Alexandria, the Red Sea, and Siwa — for travelers who want desert oases, Mediterranean coast, and unhurried time between temples.

DAY 01–02Cairo Cairo

  • Egyptian Museum / GEM, Coptic Cairo, Islamic Cairo walking tour, Khan el-Khalili.

DAY 03Giza & Saqqara Giza · Saqqara · Dashur

  • Pyramids, Sphinx, Saqqara (Djoser), Dahshur (Bent & Red Pyramids — quieter than Giza).

DAY 04Train to Alexandria Alexandria

  • Bibliotheca Alexandrina, Qaitbay Citadel, Pompey's Pillar, Catacombs of Kom el-Shoqafa.

DAY 05Alexandria & Coast Alexandria

  • Montaza Palace, seafood lunch, Corniche sunset, optional swim at Mamoura.

DAY 06Siwa Oasis Siwa

  • Drive or fly to Siwa (long road, 8h — or fly to Marsa Matrouh + transfer). Salt lakes, Oracle Temple, hot springs.
Siwa is a 2-3 day commitment. Skip if tight on time; keep if desert silence appeals.

DAY 07Siwa Siwa

  • Shali fortress, Mountain of the Dead, sunset from Dakrour.

DAY 08Fly to Luxor Luxor

  • Siwa → Cairo → Luxor (full travel day). Or Siwa → Marsa Matrouh → Cairo → Luxor.

DAY 09East Bank, Embark Luxor

  • Karnak, Luxor Temple, board cruise.

DAY 10West Bank Luxor

  • Valley of the Kings, Hatshepsut, Medinet Habu, Colossi of Memnon.

DAY 11Edfu & Kom Ombo On the Nile

  • Horus at Edfu, double temple of Kom Ombo.

DAY 12Aswan & Philae Aswan

  • Philae, High Dam, Unfinished Obelisk, felucca at sunset.

DAY 13Abu Simbel Aswan · Abu Simbel

  • Day trip by fly or convoy. Optional: Nubian dinner back in Aswan.

DAY 14Fly Home Aswan → Cairo

  • Aswan → Cairo, onward international.

🧳Packing List

  • Lightweight cotton / linen (day), warm layer (desert nights)
  • Modest cover for mosques (shoulders + knees)
  • Sun hat, SPF 50+, polarized sunglasses
  • Reusable water bottle — heat dehydrates fast
  • Comfortable walking shoes + sandals
  • Headscarf / shemagh (sand + sun + mosques)
  • Universal adapter (type C/F, 220V)
  • Small daypack with zippers
  • Anti-diarrheal, oral rehydration, basic first aid
  • Cash in small EGP notes (tipping culture, bazaars)

📅Best Time to Go

  • Oct–Apr: Cool (18–26°C), ideal for all sites.
  • May–Sep: Hot (35–45°C), low season, cheaper.
  • Ramadan: Sites stay open but hours shift, restaurants close midday.

🛂Practical Notes

  • Visa on arrival: $25 USD, or e-visa online.
  • Currency: Egyptian Pound (EGP). USD widely accepted.
  • Tipping (baksheesh) expected — small change adds up.
  • Drinks: bottled water only. Ice in hotels/restaurants is fine.
  • SIM: buy Vodafone/Orange at airport ($5 for 10GB).
